Academic Profile

Dr. Ruchi Shrivastava is a distinguished academician with over 25 years of experience in engineering education. Her academic journey began as a Teaching Assistant and has culminated in her current role as Principal at Parul Institute of Engineering and Technology - Diploma Studies, where she continues to shape the future of engineering education.

Teaching Philosophy

One of the main reasons I chose academia was the opportunity to work with and shape future generations. My teaching philosophy is built on three fundamental pillars:

  • Empowering Students - Enabling them to take control of their learning journey
  • Developing Transformative Leaders - Creating future industry leaders
  • Leveraging Technology - Using modern tools to enhance learning

My philosophy centers on student-centric learning, where students are equal partners in the educational process. I believe in facilitating learning by helping students gain the necessary skills to become active participants in their own education. Knowledge gained through active participation has lasting impact.

Teaching Methodology

To prepare engineering students for dynamic careers and help them become capable professionals, I employ various innovative teaching methods:

  • High Impact Teaching Skills using micro-teaching methodology
  • Interpersonal communication and listening skills development
  • Using analogies to simplify complex content
  • Effective handling of diverse classroom situations
  • Addressing learner diversity through adaptive teaching
  • Mapping teaching methodologies to different learning styles
  • Promoting reflective learning practices
  • Implementing innovative pedagogies

Student-Centered Approach

My overarching goal is to develop a student-centered environment that promotes active participation rather than passive learning. When designing courses, I:

  • Identify clear objectives and skills for each course
  • Develop rationales for each learning objective
  • Design course plans emphasizing critical thinking
  • Incorporate collaborative learning for real-world problem solving
  • Embed activities that develop transferable research and writing skills

Assessment and Evaluation

My evaluation approach is comprehensive and includes:

  • Collaborative projects
  • Student portfolios
  • Multimedia presentations
  • Interactive quizzes
  • Review activities
  • Traditional examinations
  • Writing assignments
  • Self-evaluation exercises

This comprehensive evaluation system incorporates both formative and summative assessments, encouraging students to engage in self-evaluation while maintaining clear connections to course objectives and industry expectations.
